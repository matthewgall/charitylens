Scoring Methodology

CharityLens provides transparency scores to help donors make informed decisions. Our methodology is open, data-driven, and based on publicly available information from the Charity Commission.

Overall Transparency Score

The overall transparency score is a composite score (0-100) calculated from four weighted components:

Efficiency Score 40%

Measures how effectively a charity uses its resources for charitable activities rather than overhead and fundraising costs.

Financial Health Score 30%

Assesses the charity's financial sustainability, reserve levels, and income trends.

Transparency Score 20%

Evaluates data completeness, filing timeliness, and public accessibility of information.

Governance Score 10%

Reviews trustee structure, policies, and governance practices.

Overall Score = (Efficiency × 0.40) + (Financial Health × 0.30) + (Transparency × 0.20) + (Governance × 0.10)

1. Efficiency Score (40% weight)

The efficiency score measures what percentage of a charity's spending goes directly to charitable activities versus administrative and fundraising costs.

Important Limitation: The Charity Commission API does not provide detailed spending breakdowns. Currently, charitable activities spend is estimated at 70% of total spending for all charities. This means efficiency scores are not accurate and should not be heavily weighted. We are working on integrating detailed annual return data for accurate figures.

Calculation

Program Spending Ratio = Charitable Activities Spend ÷ Total Spending
Efficiency Score = Program Spending Ratio × 100

Note: Currently using estimated 70% for all charities

Scoring Ranges

Program Spending Score Range Interpretation
≥ 85% 85-100 Excellent - Most funds go to programs
75-84% 75-84 Good - Strong program focus
65-74% 65-74 Fair - Acceptable overhead
< 65% 0-64 Needs Improvement - High overhead

Note: Small charities may have lower efficiency scores due to fixed costs. We consider charity size and sector when interpreting scores.

2. Financial Health Score (30% weight)

Financial health evaluates whether a charity has adequate reserves and sustainable income trends.

Components

  • Reserve Adequacy (70%): Optimal reserve level is 3-12 months of operating expenses
    • Below 3 months: May struggle with cash flow
    • 3-12 months: Healthy reserves
    • Above 12 months: May be holding excess reserves
  • Income Trends (30%): Growth or stability in income over recent years indicates financial sustainability
Months of Reserves = Reserves ÷ (Total Spending ÷ 12)

Reserve Score = {
  100 if 3 ≤ months ≤ 12
  (months/3) × 100 if months < 3
  100 - ((months-12) × 5) if months > 12
}

3. Transparency Score (20% weight)

Transparency measures how open and accessible a charity is with its information and regulatory compliance. This score uses historical filing data from the Charity Commission to assess transparency over time.

Components

  • Filing Timeliness (25%): Annual returns filed on time in the last 3 years
  • Web Presence (30%): Charity has a functioning website
  • Financial Data (20%): Current financial information is available
  • Filing Consistency (10%): No gaps in filing history over the last 5 years
  • Trustees Listed (10%): Trustee information is publicly available
  • Accounts Quality (5%): No qualified accounts (audit issues) in recent years
Transparency Score = Sum of component scores

Component breakdown:
- Filing timeliness (0-25 points): % of on-time filings × 25
- Web presence (0-30 points): Has website = 30, No website = 0
- Financial data (0-20 points): Has data = 20, Missing = 0
- Filing consistency (0-10 points): % of expected filings × 10
- Trustees listed (0-10 points): Has trustees = 10, None = 0
- Accounts quality (0-5 points): 5 - (% qualified × 5)

4. Governance Score (10% weight)

Governance assesses the charity's leadership structure and policies.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Trustee Board: Appropriate number of trustees (typically 3-12)
  • Board Diversity: Mix of skills and backgrounds
  • Trustee Information: Public disclosure of trustees
  • Policies: Evidence of governance policies and procedures
Number of Trustees Score
5-12 trustees 100
3-4 or 13-15 trustees 75
<3 or >15 trustees 50

Confidence Levels

Each score includes a confidence level indicating data quality and freshness:

High Confidence: Recent data (within 18 months), all key metrics available
Medium Confidence: Data 18-36 months old, or some metrics missing
Low Confidence: Data over 36 months old, or significant gaps in information

Interpreting Scores

Score Range Rating Meaning
80-100 Excellent Strong transparency and financial health
60-79 Good Solid performance with room for improvement
40-59 Fair Adequate but some concerns
0-39 Needs Improvement Significant transparency or financial concerns

Important Considerations

Scores are not the whole story. While our methodology provides a useful framework, it cannot capture everything about a charity's impact or effectiveness. We recommend:

  • Context Matters: Small charities may score lower due to limited resources for reporting
  • Sector Differences: Different types of charities have different cost structures
  • Impact Over Efficiency: A slightly less efficient charity may deliver more impact
  • Recent Changes: Scores reflect historical data and may not capture very recent improvements
  • Do Your Research: Use scores as a starting point, then dig deeper into charities you're interested in

Data Sources

All data is sourced from the Charity Commission for England and Wales register, which is updated regularly. We refresh our data periodically to ensure accuracy.

Feedback and Improvements

Our methodology is designed to be transparent and open to improvement. We welcome feedback from charities, donors, and sector experts. The scoring system may be refined over time based on evidence and best practices.

Questions or suggestions? Our methodology is open source and available on our license page. We encourage scrutiny and suggestions for improvement.
